The first European Bank for induced pluripotent Stem Cells

EBiSC is designed to address the increasing demand by iPSC researchers for quality-controlled, disease-relevant research grade iPSC lines, data and cell services.

EBiSC partners are establishing a centralised European banking and distribution operation that will meet growing demand from academic, clinical, industrial (biotech, pharmaceutical) researchers as well as government-funded research organisations and charities. The operation will provide a diverse array of human disease-related iPSC lines as the main component of the cell line catalogue. The central EBiSC facility will distribute existing cell lines from the catalogue and bank new lines as they are generated. The EBiSC cell line catalogue will support current and future EU-funded projects using iPSCs. It will also make future drug discovery and development more effective. It supports a diversity of strategies from the straightforward exploratory target validation to the more complex patient stratification.

During the initial “Hot Start” phase up to 64 iPS cell lines representing both disease backgrounds and normal controls will enter the EBiSC Foundational Collection. These iPS cell lines which are being expanded by the eight iPSC Centres have already been well characterised and are in most cases published.

About the EBiSC project

  • Banking and Distribution
    EBiSC’s central repository and processing operation is located at the Babraham Research Campus (close to Cambridge, UK). This facility is used to expand and quality control iPSC lines.
  • Main Work Groups
    To ensure that the EBiSC project meets its main objective in demonstrating an EU-based iPSC banking operation, different work groups have been established to carry out the complex research activities planned.
